FWIW, we're in the process of fixing it (see:
https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/Legal:Fedora_Project_Contributor_Agreement),
but the English translation (from Lawyer) of the Fedora ICLA is this:

If you contribute a work with a license on it, Fedora will use it under
that license (if it decides to use it at all). If you do not put a
license on your contribution, you give Fedora permission to use this
extremely permissive license.

We do permit hosted projects to remove the ICLA requirement though,
which Alain has done, so you shouldn't need to worry about it.
