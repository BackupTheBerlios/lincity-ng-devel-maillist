2009/2/5 Alain Baeckeroot <alain.baeckeroot at laposte.net>:
> - compared to simcity 2000 with graphics ala 3000.
>  newcomers expect lincity to behave like simcity N.
>  my guess = and so they are disapointed.
>  We should be more different, and advertise it from the start of the game.
Maybe we can add a line to the start menu which says that this game
doesn't work like Simcity or something like that.

> - sustanable development is a nice original feature :-)
Sure it is.

> - lack of a reference manual: the building's help give hints, but the new
> player lack overview of what must be done (watch stats , analyse things...)
> One want a reference book like the one in simicity 2000 !
I was thinking of starting an in-depth manual on the Lincity wiki,
which should explain every detail of the game.

> - graphics contains anachronistic stuff. (potery and cars in market).
This can be fixed by having different graphics for buildings. I
remember Jaky has already made a patch for the market, but he hasn't
uploaded it yet (or I missed something).

> - birth rate control is hard to understand.
>  Using different kind of buildings seems irrealistic to them.
>  They expect a more subblte mechanism,
>  low price -> people comes -> more jobs -> more money/taxes -> prices rise
>  -> less people come.
>  Agreed, but this is hard stuff to manage. In RL birth rate is strongly
>  linked to "richness" and political decisions to promote family planning.
>  Maybe just explaining that different kind of houses is a summary for
>  the above mechanism would be good ?
We can try explaining this mechanism better, but I still think think
it works weird. It would be better if one can handle the population
size by taxes. I prefer the way Simcity 1 does this, by letting the
user assign residential zones (3 by 3 tiles), and then the game builds
houses on them.

> - people want to control money (give less than 100% funding for social....)
>  instead of having to build/destroy buildings.
That's a good idea in my opinion. A lower fund would result in a
smaller coverage of a social building (e.g. fire stations).

> - They find difficult to understand why a building does not work. They
>  would like clear messages explaining everything.
>  Hmm, i m not sure i like to explain everything. Part of the fun is to think
>  to understand what happens, but maybe i'm biased due to knowledge of the
>  engine ?
I think we have to reckon with new players, who don't immediately
understand why a building doesn't work. Advanced players should be
able to turn help messages of if they want.

These are my thoughts about these user comments. I hope it's possible
to realize them, the only thing I can do is providing graphics, no
coding.

Greetings,
Ivar.
