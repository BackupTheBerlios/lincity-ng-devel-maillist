By: jaky_lincity

Comment:
I agree with you Ivar. The pollution of the light industry is probably to high. In the original lincity light industries had a pollution of 10/day and heavy industries 5/day.
Sometime after the last release of lincity, but before lincity-ng this was changed so that the pollution should depend on the amount of goods produced and be reduced after tech-level 100%. 
This changed the pollution to goods_produced*0.05 per day. A light industry can produce up to 1800 goods/day if it has enough power, steel and jobs. This results in 90 pollution/day.
But due to a bug this was never working and light industries didn't emit any pollution.
Last year I noticed this bug and wrote a patch to fix it, which was added to 2.0. But I always assumed (wrongly) that this had been working in the past and that this was the intended amount of pollution.

I propose to change INDUSTRY_L_POL_PER_GOOD in lin-city.h from 0.05 to 0.001. That way the maximum pollution would be 1.8/day. To cope with non-integer values for the pollution/day, the code could be changed to emit 10 times the pollution, but only every 10 days.

I think it would also be good to add a warning about pollution to the help of industries, coal power plants and coal mines.
Somehow we also need to warn the player if he has to much waste in his markets. The pollution from burning waste is quite severe and can also kill many inhabitants.

Jaky
