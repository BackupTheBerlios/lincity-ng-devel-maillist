Le lundi 21 janvier 2008, Alain Baeckeroot a écrit :
> Hi everybody
> 
> Currently different transports does not exchange material when they meet/cross
> each other. One need an intermediary market to do this.
> I find it very un-natural for track/road (when i was in Senegal is took cars
> and buses on tracks, and donkeys on roads :-)
> 
> Also, as all buildings can pick/put stuff directly from/to rail (without market
> used as a railstation) so it is hard to argue that road and rail should not
> exchange things when they cross/meet.
> 
> I would like to change this, and either:
> 
> - allow all exchanges between transport.
>   This should change very little in the gameplay, and will probably not be
>   noticeable for old games, but would prevent some trouble for new players.
> 
> - allow exchanges only between track and roads
>   AND
>   change behavior of rail and force use of markets as rail-stations (of
>   course except for the building who need connections)
    I forgot to add:
    road and track could cross railways like power lines do, i mean one road
    can continue on the other side of the railway (like in real life)

>   This is a bigger change, but it can give better control on the transport
>   for example with only one market at each end of a railway, you know what
>   happens. I guess that with all my current games this would change nothing
>   as i have many markets along railways....
>   This need some translations changes too in rail.xml and market.xml
> 
> What do you think ?
