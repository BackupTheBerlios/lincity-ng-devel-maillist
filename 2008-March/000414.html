Le mardi 4 mars 2008, Ivar ten Cate a écrit :
> I have also one thing which can be added to the game: water irrigation in
> deserts. Water irrigation
> can be a good way for the user to get rid of deserts. I think it should be
> something like this:
> the user can build a water irrigation system (which I can draw :) ) on a
> desert tile. Of course
> a water irrigation system costs money and it should be placed near a water
> well or river. After some
> months, the desert tile turns in a grass tile and the water system goes away
> (automatically).
> In that way a user can turn entire deserts in lucrative ground, on which he
> can build parks, farms
> and other stuff.

Maybe "desert" is a too hard word, and "bare ground" would be better.

I don't like the idea of suppressing desert, because real desert cannot be
turn into a rich land. It is desert because there is little water and
nearly no soil. Richs farm are where there is water, good climate for the crop,
and a lot of good soil, no matter how much technology and money you invest.

But i agree water management is currently very simplistic, and i plan to add
features in 2.+

Ideas and contributions are of course welcome.
